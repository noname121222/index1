Every dish has its own story, traditions, and, undoubtedly, its admirers. For me, such a unique and beloved dish is pilaf. My fascination with pilaf began in childhood when I watched my mother prepare this meal for the whole family. For me, the process of making pilaf is a whole experience. I start by selecting the ingredients: aromatic long-grain rice, fresh lamb, and vibrant vegetables. I chop the onions, carrots, and meat. When I sauté the onions until they turn golden, their sweet aroma fills the kitchen. Then I add the carrots, which not only add color but also sweetness to the pilaf.

Once the meat is browned to a crispy golden color, I add the spices — cumin, turmeric, and paprika. Their aromas intertwine, filling the space with a delightful scent. The next step is to add the rice, which I rinse beforehand to remove excess starch. I carefully distribute the rice over the meat and vegetables, then pour in the water.

I love preparing pilaf for myself during moments when I crave a delicious and unusual dish. For me, pilaf is not just a meal; it’s a whole story reflecting the warmth and care that go into its preparation.
